## üé≠ Core Character

### Physical Appearance
- **Build:** Chubby, exaggerated proportions, big round belly that strains his shirt buttons
- **Hair:** White/grey (getting older, doesn't care)
- **Face:** Simple cartoon features - dots for eyes, wide expressive mouth, rosy cheeks
- **Attire:** 
  - White captain's hat with gold anchor emblem (bought himself, not earned)
  - Bright blue Hawaiian shirt with big orange/red hibiscus flowers (always too tight)
  - Khaki shorts (often stained, pockets full of random items)
  - Flip flops (worn, possibly mismatched)
  - Gold watch (expensive but doesn't know how to use it)
- **Accessories:** Cigar occasionally when story warrants it (not default)

### Personality Traits
- **Overconfident:** Believes he knows everything about yachting (he doesn't)
- **Entitled:** "DO YOU KNOW WHO I AM?!" - expects special treatment everywhere
- **Oblivious:** Never realizes he's the problem
- **Blame-Shifter:** Everything is the crew's fault, weather's fault, marina's fault
- **Frugal (Cheap):** Complains about fuel prices, crew salaries, ice costs
- **Impulsive:** Makes decisions without thinking, then doubles down when wrong

### Speech Patterns
- **Volume:** Loud, especially when wrong
- **Catchphrases:**
  - "DO YOU KNOW WHO I AM?!"
  - "I've got a RESERVATION!"
  - "Safety briefing? I bought the boat, didn't I?"
  - "Someone's getting fired today!"
  - "That's what insurance is for!"
- **Style:** Demanding, declarative, never asks politely

## üë• Supporting Characters

### The Captain (Name: TBD)
- **Build:** Fit, professional, long-suffering
- **Appearance:** Proper uniform, always neat, weathered but dignified
- **Personality:** Professional, patient, deadpan, the straight man to TR's antics
- **Role:** Tries to keep TR alive despite his best efforts
- **Expression:** Usually worried, occasionally shocked, rarely amused

### Crew Members
- **Build:** Fit, healthy, professional
- **Appearance:** Proper uniforms, neat appearance
- **Personality:** Competent, professional, silently judging TR
- **Role:** Do the actual work while TR takes credit

### Guests/Charter Clients
- **Build:** Mixed - can be fat, skinny, attractive, etc. depending on story
- **Appearance:** Varies by context
- **Personality:** Often horrified by TR's behavior
- **Role:** Witnesses to the chaos, sometimes victims

### Marina Staff/Officials
- **Build:** Professional, varies
- **Appearance:** Uniforms or professional attire
- **Personality:** Bureaucratic, unimpressed by TR's demands
- **Role:** The immovable object to TR's unstoppable entitlement

## üé® Visual Style Guide

### Art Style
- **Format:** Comic book panels, Sunday comics style
- **Lines:** Bold black outlines
- **Colors:** Flat, bright, vibrant (not realistic shading)
- **Proportions:** Exaggerated for humor
- **Expression:** Big, readable facial expressions

### TR Consistency Checklist
Before generating any panel, verify:
- [ ] White/grey hair
- [ ] Captain's hat with anchor emblem
- [ ] Blue Hawaiian shirt (orange/red flowers)
- [ ] Khaki shorts
- [ ] Big round belly
- [ ] Flip flops
- [ ] Chubby build (not just "big")
- [ ] Simple cartoon face (dots for eyes, wide mouth)
- [ ] Cigar only if story requires it

### Crew/Captain Consistency
- [ ] Fit, healthy build
- [ ] Professional uniforms
- [ ] Neat appearance
- [ ] Exasperated/worried expressions

## üìù Story Categories

### TR's Greatest Hits (Recurring Themes)
1. **Navigation Disasters:** Getting lost, ignoring charts, GPS confusion
2. **Safety Violations:** Skipping briefings, overloading, ignoring weather
3. **Crew Management:** Micromanaging, blame-shifting, impossible demands
4. **Marina Mayhem:** Docking disasters, entitlement with staff, fee disputes
5. **Provisioning Problems:** Cheapness, weird food requests, running out of essentials
6. **Guest Relations:** Making guests uncomfortable, showing off, accidents
7. **Maintenance Mishaps:** Ignoring problems, DIY disasters, mechanic confusion

### Humor Types That Work
- **Irony:** TR says one thing, does the opposite
- **Understatement:** Captain's deadpan reactions
- **Visual Gags:** Size differences, physical comedy
- **Escalation:** Small problem ‚Üí TR makes it worse ‚Üí Chaos
- **Schadenfreude:** Watching TR get his comeuppance

## üí° Writing Tips for Submissions

### What Makes a Good TR Story
1. **Specific detail:** Exact location, boat size, situation
2. **TR's voice:** Can you hear him shouting?
3. **Visual humor:** Can you see the scene?
4. **Irony:** TR thinks he's right, clearly isn't
5. **Relatability:** Other crew members have been there

### What to Avoid
- Too much setup - get to the TR moment quickly
- Complex technical details - focus on the human interaction
- TR being competent - he's not
- Happy endings - TR rarely wins
